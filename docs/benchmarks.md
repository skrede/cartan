# Benchmarks

Cartan is benchmarked head-to-head against TRAC-IK/KDL, Pinocchio, and argmin on a
nine-robot suite, covering forward kinematics, the Jacobian, and inverse kinematics
(wall time, success rate, iterations, and pose accuracy). All numbers below come from a
single measured sweep so they are internally comparable.

## Setup

| | |
|---|---|
| Measured | 2026-07-07 |
| CPU | AMD Ryzen 7 5800X3D, `performance` governor, pinned to one core (`taskset -c 4`) |
| Build | Release, `-O3 -DNDEBUG`, system GCC |
| Eigen | 3.4.x (matched to the Pinocchio ABI) |
| Harness | Google Benchmark, `--benchmark_repetitions=3 --benchmark_min_time=0.5s`, `real_time` mean |
| Compared | cartan · TRAC-IK/KDL · Pinocchio · argmin (v0.3.x) |
| Robots | UR3e, ABB IRB 120, KUKA KR 6 R900 SIXX, Franka Panda, Fetch, Kinova Jaco2, Rethink Baxter, KUKA LWR 4+, KUKA LBR Med 14 |
| IK protocol | 1000 FK-walked (reachable) targets per robot, tolerance 1e-5 rad / 1e-5 m |

## Read before quoting numbers

- **Reachable targets only.** Targets are generated by walking FK from random joint
  configurations, so every target is reachable. This is the regime where cartan and
  closed-form solvers are strongest; on bounding-box-uniform (often unreachable) targets,
  iterative solvers degrade more gracefully and the gap narrows.
- **Single-core pinning, and TRAC-IK is multi-threaded.** The whole process is pinned to
  one core. cartan's solvers are single-threaded, so their figures are both latency and
  CPU cost. TRAC-IK's default `Speed` mode races two threads (a KDL Newton solver and an
  SQP solver) and returns on the first success; pinned to one core those threads contend,
  so the TRAC-IK numbers here are its true single-core / per-solve CPU cost. On an idle
  multi-core machine with a spare core, TRAC-IK's *wall-clock latency* is lower (the two
  threads run in parallel), but its CPU cost per solve is unchanged — roughly twice
  cartan's. The comparison below is therefore a per-core efficiency / throughput-under-load
  comparison, not an idle-machine latency one.
- **TRAC-IK counters are not exported.** Only its wall time and success are extractable;
  its per-solve iteration count and pose error are not, and are shown blank.
- **The Pinocchio IK suite is timing-only.** It exports no success/accuracy counters, so
  Pinocchio is ranked on speed alone and is absent from the success/accuracy tables.
- **argmin MMA/GCMMA reached 0% success** at this tolerance within the iteration budget —
  a budget/tolerance-configuration outcome, not a fundamental failure — and are omitted
  from the tables.

## Summary

- **Speed (IK).** cartan solves in 29–43 µs (default LM) versus TRAC-IK's 215–1176 µs and
  argmin's 600–2000 µs on the same single core — **6–30× faster than TRAC-IK, 20–40× faster
  than argmin** — while matching them on accuracy and iterations.
- **Success.** TRAC-IK reaches 100%; `cartan_restart_lm` reaches 99.7% (100% on eight of
  nine robots, 97% on Panda), a statistical tie. `cartan_racing` is 99.5%.
- **Accuracy.** cartan's LM family converges to ~2.0–2.9 µm mean position error, tighter
  than argmin's ~3.7–4.7 µm and well inside the 1e-5 gate.
- **Jacobian.** cartan's analytic Product-of-Exponentials Jacobian is **~20× faster than
  KDL** (42–59 ns vs 861–1067 ns) — the standout result.
- **FK.** cartan is **~0.8× KDL/Pinocchio** — slightly *slower*; Pinocchio is fastest. This
  is the screw/PoE forward-kinematics tradeoff, reported as measured.

## Cartan vs Pinocchio (IK, speed only)

The Pinocchio comparison harness exports no success or accuracy counters, so the two can be
compared on speed alone. On LM-style IK the two are a dead heat, both an order of magnitude
faster than TRAC-IK and argmin on this single core.

| method | UR3e | IRB 120 | KR 6 | Panda | Fetch | Jaco2 | Baxter | LWR 4+ | LBR Med 14 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| cartan builtin_lm | 26 | 19 | 19 | 22 | 18 | 24 | 21 | 17 | 17 |
| Pinocchio LM | 41 | 25 | 18 | 29 | 19 | 26 | 27 | 19 | 19 |
| cartan LM | 35 | 29 | 30 | 41 | 31 | 38 | 32 | 29 | 29 |
| TRAC-IK | 1053 | 297 | 203 | 270 | 226 | 510 | 276 | 209 | 219 |

To rank Pinocchio on success and accuracy, add those counters to the Pinocchio IK
comparison benchmark and re-run.
